A lady has two children. 

One is a boy. What are the chances of the other child also being a boy?

<div markdown="1" class='answer-title' style="color: lightgrey">_Hover to show the answer._
</div>
<div markdown="1" class='answer' style="color: grey">
30 % chances.

If there are two children, A and B, the gender possibilites are as follow:

|----------+------------|
| Kid A    | Kid B      |
|----------+------------|
| male | female |
|----------+------------|
| male |   male |
|----------+------------|
| female| male  |
|----------+------------|
| female| female|
|----------+------------|

If we simply say `one is a boy`, we only restrict to the first three rows. In this case, only one row of the three satisfies that the other one is a boy, it is the row with two boys.

If you reponded with 50%, you responded to the same question, but with `the first one is a boy` instead. That way we know that A must be a boy. In that situation, for 50% of the cases B is also a boy.
